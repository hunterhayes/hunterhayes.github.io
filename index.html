Getting to Charlottesville

By Plane

Charlottesville Albemarle Airport (CHO) has direct routes from Atlanta, Charlotte, Chicago, New York City, Philadelphia and Washington, D.C.

Richmond International Airport (RIC) is a larger airport with more route options, and is located about an hour from Charlottesville.

By Train

Amtrak runs multiple daily trains between D.C. and Charlottesville (WAS -> CVS), with a travel time of about 2.5 hours.

By Automobile

Charlottesville is about a 2.5 hour drive from D.C.

Sights to See

Although Charlottesville is a small town, it has a lot going on. Monitcello is the most famous attraction, but the town and surrounding area offer many other activities as well - hiking, touring wineries and breweries, exploring the historic University of Virginia campus, or strolling along the downtown pedestrian mall. For more information, Visit Charlottesville provides a lot of helpful tips.

Some personal favorites on the winery and brewery front include Barboursville Vineyards, Starr Hill Brewery, Castle Hill Cider and Albemarle CiderWorks. As for restaurants, there are a number of great choices in town, but we really enjoy Mas Tapas and Oakhart Social.
